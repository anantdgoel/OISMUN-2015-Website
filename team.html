About

Brinda Mehra

Dear Delegates,
Welcome to the Human Rights Committee at the third OIS MUN. My name is Brinda Mehra and I will be co-chairing HRC, along with Smiti Modi and Aish Sandeep. This will be my second MUN conference overall and the first one as a Chair.
Alongside the OISMUN, I also enjoy reading books from a variety of eras and cultures, learning new languages, baking, photography and when I can find the time, having marathon TV watching sessions of the Mentalist or Castle.
In my very first MUN, which was at OIS, I was in the delegate of Sri Lanka in DISEC. The biggest thing I took away from this experience, apart from the understanding of how the UN works is that to have a successful committee, we require participation from all delegates. There is no sense in keeping quiet when the topic chosen affects your country, either directly or indirectly.
The topics that we have chosen for HRC this year are events that do not get as much coverage as they should but balance precariously on the thin line that divides the observence of human rights from their violations. These topics are harsh and real, and affect the world in several different ways.
In order to have a productive debate and arrive on good resolutions, Smiti, Aish and I expect each one of you to prepare and participate fully. For all first time delegates, please remember that there is no use of keeping quiet if you have something to say and do not be afraid of making mistakes.If you really can’t gather up your courage, remember, that one of your chairs is a first timer as well.
Here’s to a fruitful MUN conference for all of us.
Best of luck, delegates.
